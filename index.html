Water Pollution

Water pollution refers to the contamination of oceans, seas, rivers, and other water bodies by harmful substances that negatively impact marine ecosystems and human health. It occurs due to various sources such as plastic waste, oil spills, industrial chemicals, agricultural runoff, and untreated sewage. These pollutants reduce water quality, decrease oxygen levels, and disrupt the natural balance of aquatic life. Marine organisms like fish, turtles, and coral reefs suffer greatly as toxic substances accumulate in their bodies, leading to diseases and even extinction in severe cases. In addition, polluted water can enter the human food chain, causing serious health issues. Addressing water pollution requires global awareness, sustainable practices, proper waste management, and strict environmental regulations to protect marine biodiversity and ensure a healthy future for our planet.

Sonar Types

Sonar (Sound Navigation and Ranging) is a technology that uses sound waves to detect and locate objects underwater. It is widely used in marine research, navigation, and defense systems. There are different types of sonar systems, such as active sonar, which emits sound pulses and listens for echoes, and passive sonar, which only listens to sounds produced by objects like ships or marine animals. Sonar helps scientists study ocean depths, map the seafloor, track marine species, and monitor underwater activities. It plays a crucial role in ocean exploration and contributes significantly to marine conservation and safety.
